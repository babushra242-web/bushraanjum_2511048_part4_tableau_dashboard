# Business Insights – Chart Type Selection

##  Sales Trend View
**Chart Type:** Line Chart  
**Reasoning:** Line charts are best for showing changes over time. This highlights seasonality, growth, and long-term trends in sales.

---

##  Regional Performance View
**Chart Type:** Map + Bar Chart  
**Reasoning:** Maps provide geographic context, while bar charts allow precise comparison of sales/profit across regions or states. Together, they show both spatial and numerical performance.

---

##  Category Profitability View
**Chart Type:** Bar Chart or Treemap  
**Reasoning:** Bar charts clearly compare profit margins across categories. Treemaps are useful for visualizing hierarchical relationships (Category → Sub-Category) and relative contribution to profit.

---

##  Customer Segment View
**Chart Type:** Bar Chart or Highlight Table  
**Reasoning:** Bar charts make segment comparisons easy to interpret. Highlight tables add color-coded performance indicators for quick identification of high-value or high-risk segments.

---

##  Shipping Performance View
**Chart Type:** Bar Chart or Table  
**Reasoning:** Bar charts show differences in shipping mode performance. Tables allow detailed breakdowns of delivery delays by bucket, providing operational clarity.

---

##  Discount vs Profit View
**Chart Type:** Scatter Plot  
**Reasoning:** Scatter plots reveal the relationship between discount percentage and profit. This helps identify whether discounts are eroding margins or driving profitable volume.

---

##  Return Analysis View
**Chart Type:** Bar Chart or Highlight Table  
**Reasoning:** Bar charts show return rates by category, segment, or region. Highlight tables provide a quick visual cue for areas with high return risk.

---

---

## ✅ Business Impact
By aligning chart types with business questions, the dashboard ensures leadership can quickly interpret patterns, compare performance, and act on insights without confusion.
